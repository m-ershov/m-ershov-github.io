Course outline and prerequisites

This course is an introduction to the theory of error-correcting codes, that is, codes designed to provide reliable transmission of information in the presence of noise. The topics will include a general theory of linear codes, a detailed discussion of some particular classes of codes (e.g. Hamming codes, BCH codes), and the associated encoding and decoding algorithms. Specific (real life) applications of codes will also be briefly discussed. A few lectures will be devoted to developing necessary algebraic background for the course, mainly basic theory of finite fields. The precise selection of topics will depend on the interests of the students taking the course and may not be determined until a few weeks after the start of the semester.

The official prerequisites for Math 4452 are Math 3351 Elementary Linear Algebra and Math 3354 Survey of Algebra. The linear algebra prerequisite is essential and will be assumed. If you have not taken a prior course in abstract algebra, it is possible to take 4452 if you are willing to learn basic number theory and modular arithmetic (roughly first 4 weeks of 3354) on your own (see, e.g. Lectures 1-9 of my 3354 notes). In addition, a substantial prior exposure to proof-based mathematics will be expected.
